Cert I Hospitality (Kitchen Operations)

Holmesglen Institute of TAFE and Didasko - Ross Digby

  • Story
  • Map (PDF 47KB)
  • Text transcript of digital story

Approach

The Certificate I in Hospitality (Kitchen Operations) is a blended delivery model using self-paced materials, group sessions and practical classes. It uses a procedural learning approach suited to younger learners enrolled in the VET in the VCE Hospitality program who have few underpinning skills or previous knowledge of the processes involved in the hospitality industry.

View the design

In the story Ross describes how the resources are integrated into the program. He describes some of the challenges of meeting the needs of a large number of younger learners in a VET program.

The learning design map describes delivery of a unit of competency of the course.

Features

  • blended delivery, integrates face-to-face and online
  • procedural approach - assists younger learners
  • supports self-access learning
  • ensures consistency of program delivery across multiple sites.
 

Further details

Course Certificate II in Hospitality (Kitchen Operations)
Provider Holmesglen Institute of TAFE
Location

Based at Moorabbin Campus, Melbourne, Victoria

Delivered across Melbourne’s South Eastern Region
Staff Ross Digby – Manager, Hospitality Programs
Competencies
  • THHHCO01B – Develop and update Hospitality Industry knowledge
  • THHCOR01B – Work with colleagues and customers
  • THHCOR02B – Work in a socially diverse environment
  • THHCOR03B – Follow health safety and security procedures
  • THHGHS01B – Follow workplace hygiene procedures
  • THHBKA01B – Organise and prepare food
  • THHBKA02B – Present food
  • THHBKA04B – Clean and maintain kitchen premises
  • THHBCC01B – Use basic methods of cookery
Training package THH02 Hospitality Training Package
Courseware Didasko CD-ROM
Features
  • Blended delivery approach for the VET in the VCE Hospitality program
  • Assists younger learners develop underpinning skills and knowledge
  • Supports self-directed learning
  • Ensures consistency of program delivery across multiple sites
Comments

“Due to the young average age of the learners (16 – 17 years of age) most had very little knowledge or experience of the hospitality industry – and as such were developing skills and knowledge from a very low base…
One of the main features of the learning approach using the Didasko package is that learners are able to test/assess the underpinning skills and knowledge through self assessments in the package …” Ross Digby
