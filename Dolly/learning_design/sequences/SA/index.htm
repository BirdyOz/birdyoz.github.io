Cert II in IT (Computer Operations)

TAFE South Australia - Kathy Schultz

  • Story
  • Map (PDF 44KB)
  • Text transcript of digital story

Approach

Certificate II in Information Technology (Computer Opertions) uses both virtual and real-life simulated workplaces in a blended learning approach to give learners access to workplace policies and guidelines and practical workplace tasks.

View the design

In the story Kathy describes how she delivers the course, talks about some of the challenges of providing workplace assessment for learners not currently engaged in the workplace and about the solutions she is working on.

The learning design map shows two weeks of the course.

Features

  • online virtual workplace
  • simulated workplace for practical assessment
  • blended learning
  • face-to-face classes.

Features

Comments

“The best way to assess skills is in a real life workplace.
Since most of our learners don’t have access to a workplace we have created one.
'Leprechaun Computing’ is a simulated workplace (virtual and real life) which assumes learners are entry level technicians and gives them appropriate tasks to assess their skills.”
This learning design combines an online virtual workplace where learners can access typical workplace guidelines and policies, simulated real life workplaces for practical assessment and face-to-face classes for skills and knowledge development.
