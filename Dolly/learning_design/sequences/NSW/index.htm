Dip. HR Management (Information Systems)

Sutherland College of TAFE NSW - Jenni Harding

  • Story
  • Map (PDF 44KB)
  • Text transcript of digital story

Approach

The Diploma of Human Resource Management (Information Systems) is a project-based approach to e-learning. This blended learning model offers learners a choice between using their own organisation or the simulated workplace 'STAR Industries' available with the HRM Diploma Flexible Learning Toolbox.

View the design

In the story Jenni describes how the course is delivered, and how learners use the Janison shell for communication and to access the the HRM Diploma Toolbox learning materials.

The learning design map shows two weeks of the course.

Features

  • blended learning approach
  • project-based
  • simulated workplace or own workplace
  • online communication and facilitation
  • e-learning materials from HRM Diploma Toolbox.
 

Further details

Course Human Resources Management Diploma
Provider Sydney Institute of TAFE
Location Sutherland College of TAFE, Sydney NSW
Module delivered online and face-to-face
Staff Jenni Harding – Head Teacher, Business Services Sutherland College of TAFENSW
Competency BSBHR502A Manage human resource management information systems
Training package BSB01 Business Services Training Package
Courseware Online module in Janison LMS. Human Resources Toolbox
Features
  • Blended learning including – face to face, handouts, Toobox, virtual communication with forums, chats and announcements, simulation, application to workplaces, group work
  • Virtual simulated organisation, 'STAR Industries'
  • Task-based project leading to assessment
  • Assessment based on tasks built into the Toolbox
Comments

“This has been a great way of learning and teaching at the same time. The Toolbox allowed me to focus on learning, and adapting the resources to the learner’s needs rather than developing materials from scratch.

Probably the best thing about having readings, research, reviews and other learning activities is that you have more time as a teacher for feedback to learners – through emails, forums and chats in particular, and on the phone. Learners have been very positive about the methods of learning, although two comments stand out:
