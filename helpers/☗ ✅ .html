• Decide on cut-off criteria

    Decide on cut-off criteria for eligibility for Learning Intervention Support at your school.

  • Select standardised screeners

    Select standardised screeners which you will use to test students upon school entry e.g. the PAT-Maths, NAPLAN. Key areas to target may include:

    1. Spelling
    2. Reading
    3. Writing
    4. Mathematical Ability
    5. Visual Processing e.g. Ravens Progressive Matrices.

  • Select more comprehensive standardised tests

    Select more comprehensive standardised tests you will use if students are shown to be eligible for support in the key areas mentioned. This will answer the questions of:

    1. What are the key areas of concern?
    2. What are the causative factors?
    3. What are the strengths this student has that we can drawn on to support them in their areas of challenge?

  • Select formative (baseline & outcome) tests

    Select formative (baseline & outcome) tests (often embedded within intervention programs) which will allow you determine where the student is positioned in a key area on the developmental continuum (that is, their "growth point' or 'zone of proximal development'), and create an evidence based intervention. This will enable you to answer the question: What is the learner ready to learn?
