Tour 1: Get started in e-learning

Find out about the main types, some key tools and delivery methods, and take a detailed look inside a sample course (allow two hours).

Itinerary

Stops on this tour

What to do

What you'll need from the travel pack
1. Check the types of e-learning

E-learning is quite hard to define, but we need to have some idea of what we're talking about. A useful approach is to look at the main types common in organisations and training providers.

  • Take a look at the resource describing the Types of e-learning.
  • Complete the Which types are you? worksheet to clarify which areas of e-learning are relevant to your organisation or setting.

Types of e-learning (Word doc 69KB)          

Which types are you? (Word doc 109KB)

 
2. View a range of e-learning strategies

Now you've got an idea of the scope of e-learning, zoom in to have a look at what e-learning looks like in action.

  • Go to the Gallery of strategies and select the View all tab and select one of e-learning strategies listed .
  • View the Samples at the top and then scroll down to find out why, how and with what.
  • Now complete the Gallery webquest.

Gallery webquest quiz (Word doc 60KB)
3. Investigate a sample e-learning design

Strategies are fine, but how do you put them together to make an e-learning course?

  • Click to the Learning design tab and read the Overview page.
  • Click the Sample designs tab and select the first design, the Medical Terminology course.
  • Click on the Story and listen to Lyn’s description of how the course works, then complete the Building an online community checklist.
  • Finally, complete the Learning design webquest quiz.
